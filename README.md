# Tangible for Claude

**Talk to real customers. Get answers in days — without leaving Claude.**

Tangible helps founders and product leaders run custom research from a normal Claude conversation. Describe what you want to learn, Tangible drafts a real study in about **3 minutes**, **recruits the right people** to answer it, and brings results back in **24–72 hours**. Claude helps you read what they said in plain English.

No research background required. No spreadsheets. No survey software to learn.

Tangible supports two kinds of research through Claude today:

1. **Ask a Customer** — open-ended listening. Hear what customers think, need, and struggle with in their own words.
2. **Concept Test** — structured comparison. See which landing page, ad, design, or mockup wins when you have 2+ options to show people.

## Get started (Claude on the web)

1. Open **[claude.ai](https://claude.ai)** and sign in.
2. Go to **Settings → Connectors** (or **Integrations**, depending on your account).
3. Choose **Add connector** or **Add custom connector**.
4. Paste this URL: **`https://mcp.tangiblehq.ai`**
5. Save. Claude will open Tangible in your browser the first time — sign in there once. After that, you're connected.

That's it. No API keys, no setup wizard, no IT ticket.

## What this is for (and what to expect)

Tangible is built for **decisions you can't make from gut feel alone** — when you need to hear from real customers or prospects before you ship a message, a page, or a product bet.

**What you do:** describe your question and who should answer it. Claude and Tangible handle the research design.

**What Tangible does:** drafts the study, **recruits a matched audience** (you don't need your own customer list), collects responses, and generates a report.

**What you get back:** themes in customers' own words — and for concept tests, a clear read on which option wins.

**Typical timeline:**

| Stage | How long |
|---|---|
| Draft your study | ~3 minutes |
| Review and launch | A few minutes in the Tangible app |
| Recruit respondents | 24–72 hours |
| Read results in Claude | As soon as responses are in |

**What to expect from good research:**

- You ask **one focused question** — not everything at once.
- You describe **who should answer** in plain language — role, company type, geography. Tangible finds people who match.
- You get **directional truth**, not a census. A well-run study tells you what patterns to trust and what to test next.
- **Launch always happens in the Tangible app** — you'll see pricing before anything goes live.

## The two ways to use Tangible

### 1. Ask a Customer — listen before you decide

Use this when you want to **learn**, not compare. You're exploring a problem, validating a hunch, or hearing how people describe their world today.

Tangible shapes the study around **what you're trying to learn**. These are the four most common starting points — tell Claude which one fits, or describe your goal in your own words:

#### Customer discovery
*Talk to the market before you assume the problem.*

Best when you're exploring a new space, validating a hunch, or checking whether a pain point is real — **before** you have strong product-market signal.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who to talk to** | Prospects and people in your target market (Tangible recruits a matched audience) |
| **What you'll learn** | Whether the problem exists, how people cope today, what they'd need, how they describe the category |
| **Example prompt** | *"We're exploring whether finance teams actually struggle with month-end close — I want to hear from ops and finance leaders at mid-market companies, in their own words, before we build anything."* |

#### Onboarding & first impressions
*Find out where new users get stuck.*

Best when activation is flat, trial conversion is weak, or you suspect the first experience doesn't deliver the "aha" you intended.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who to talk to** | Recent signups or people early in their journey with your product |
| **What you'll learn** | First impressions, where onboarding friction shows up, gap between what they expected and what happened |
| **Example prompt** | *"I want to talk to people who signed up in the last 30 days about what confused them in their first week — especially where they stalled before getting value."* |

#### Product feedback
*Get focused input on a product, feature, or area.*

Best when you know *where* in the product you want signal — a feature, workflow, or area you're iterating on — and want structured open-ended feedback from people who use it.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who to talk to** | Active users of the product or area in question |
| **What you'll learn** | What's working, what's painful, what they'd prioritize next, how they actually use it |
| **Example prompt** | *"I want feedback from active users on our reporting module — what's working, what's frustrating, and what they'd want us to fix first."* |

#### Voice of customer
*Capture how customers describe you — in their language.*

Best when you need the words customers use (for messaging, positioning, sales enablement) or want to understand why they chose you and what they compare you to.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who to talk to** | Current customers — optionally split by segment, tier, or tenure |
| **What you'll learn** | How they describe their needs, how they think about your category, why they picked you, what they'd miss if you disappeared |
| **Example prompt** | *"I want to hear from our mid-market customers — how they describe what we do, why they chose us, and what they'd use if we didn't exist."* |

**How it works:** you describe the goal and audience. Tangible drafts a short study, **recruits people who fit**, and collects open-ended responses (quick survey or short AI voice interview). Claude summarizes what people said.

**Default rule:** if you're not comparing specific options side-by-side, start here — and pick whichever preset above is closest to your decision.

### 2. Concept Test — compare options and pick a winner

Use this when you have **2 or more concrete things to show** and need a buyer-real signal before you commit budget or ship.

Share your concepts as links or files — landing pages, Figma URLs, images, PDFs, short videos, or text descriptions. Tangible shows each option to real people in your target audience and tells you which one wins.

These are the most common concept-test starting points:

#### Landing page or website
*Pick the page that communicates best before you launch.*

Compare 2–5 homepage variants, pricing pages, or key landing pages. Best when copy and layout are built and you need to know which version lands with buyers.

| | |
|---|---|
| **What to bring** | URLs to live or staging pages, Figma links, or screenshots of each variant |
| **What you'll learn** | Which page is clearer, more credible, or more compelling — and why |
| **Example prompt** | *"I have two homepage versions for HR leaders — here are the links. Which one makes our value prop clearer before we launch?"* |

#### Ad or campaign creative
*See which creative stops the scroll.*

Compare ad concepts, social creative, email hero visuals, or campaign directions before you spend media budget.

| | |
|---|---|
| **What to bring** | Image URLs, video links, or mockups of each ad concept |
| **What you'll learn** | Which creative feels most relevant, trustworthy, or attention-grabbing to your audience |
| **Example prompt** | *"Here are three LinkedIn ad concepts for VP Sales — which one would make them stop and read?"* |

#### Packaging or retail concept
*Choose the direction that wins on shelf or online.*

Compare packaging designs, retail displays, or product presentation — especially when visual identity drives the purchase decision.

| | |
|---|---|
| **What to bring** | Images or PDFs of each packaging direction |
| **What you'll learn** | Which design feels premium, clear, or "for me" — and which you'd put in front of buyers |
| **Example prompt** | *"We're choosing between two packaging directions for retail rollout — I want buyers in our category to react to both before we print."* |

#### Product or feature mockup
*Pressure-test a feature, flow, or product concept before you build.*

Compare UI mockups, feature concepts, product descriptions, or prototype flows when the idea is visual but not yet shipped.

| | |
|---|---|
| **What to bring** | Figma links, screenshots, short demo videos, or written concept descriptions (2–5 options) |
| **What you'll learn** | Which concept feels most useful, understandable, or worth paying for |
| **Example prompt** | *"I have two onboarding flow mockups in Figma — which feels simpler to a first-time user in our ICP?"* |

**How it works:** you share the concepts and describe the audience. Tangible builds a comparison study, **recruits respondents**, and shows you which concept wins — with themes and quotes explaining why.

**Important:** concept tests need real stimuli to compare. If you only have a vague question and no options to show, use **Ask a Customer** instead.

## How to talk to customers (and actually learn)

### Start with a decision, not a survey

Before you create a study, finish this sentence:

> *"If I knew \_\_\_\_ from customers, I would \_\_\_\_."*

Examples that fit **Ask a Customer:**

- *"If I knew which onboarding step confuses people, I would simplify it."* → **Onboarding & first impressions**
- *"If I knew how buyers describe our category, I would rewrite our homepage."* → **Voice of customer** or **Customer discovery**
- *"If I knew whether this pain point is real, I would prioritize the roadmap."* → **Customer discovery**
- *"If I knew what active users hate about reporting, I would fix it first."* → **Product feedback**

Examples that fit **Concept Test:**

- *"If I knew which homepage made our value prop clearer, I would ship that one."* → **Landing page**
- *"If I knew which ad concept felt more trustworthy, I would put budget behind it."* → **Ad / campaign creative**

Tell Claude that sentence. It picks the right study type.

### Describe your audience like you'd explain it to a colleague

You don't pick from a dropdown. Just say who matters:

- *"Product managers at mid-size B2B SaaS companies in the US"*
- *"People who signed up for our trial but never activated"*
- *"HR leaders at companies with 200–2,000 employees"*

Tangible turns that into targeting and **recruits people who fit** — you don't need to email your own list or hunt for respondents yourself.

### Read results for patterns, not perfection

When responses come in, ask Claude:

- *"What's the headline finding?"*
- *"What surprised you?"*
- *"What would you do next based on this?"*

For concept tests, also ask: *"Which option won, and why?"*

Look for **repeated themes** and **strong quotes**. Tangible redacts personal details before anything reaches Claude.

## Good to know

- **Tangible recruits the audience for you** — describe who you want; matched respondents participate through Tangible's panel. You don't need your own email list.
- **Ask a Customer is the default** for open-ended questions. Concept Test is for when you have specific options to compare.
- **Launch happens in the Tangible app** — Claude helps you draft and refine; you always review cost and confirm launch in your browser.
- **Pricing is transparent** — you see the line item on the review page before anything goes live.
- **Your customer data stays yours** — personal details are removed before results come back into Claude.
- **Best fit:** founders and PMs who need to listen to customers or pick between concrete concepts fast.
